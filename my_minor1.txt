Statistics (Minor)
Program Description

Students who pursue a minor in statistics will develop a comprehensive skill set in quantitative and analytical methods applicable across disciplines. The curriculum covers data analysis and modeling techniques including descriptive and inferential statistics, regression modeling, experimental design, and practical applications of statistics. Students will learn to effectively visualize and communicate results through charts, graphs, and clear presentations. They will hone their quantitative reasoning abilities through critical thinking, hypothesis testing, and applying statistical methods to solve real-world problems while understanding the strengths and limitations of analyses. Additionally, students will gain computing skills using statistical software. In our data-driven world, a minor in statistics is an excellent companion to any major.
Program Code
233
Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs)
Develop foundational skills in mathematics.
Demonstrate proficiency in basic statistical techniques.
Produce code for statistical analyses.
Create appropriate visualizations of data.
Justify the applicability of a statistical procedure to a given situation.
Design statistically sound experiments and analyze the results.
Apply statistical modeling techniques to univariate and multivariate scenarios.
Communicate statistical results to a specific audience.
